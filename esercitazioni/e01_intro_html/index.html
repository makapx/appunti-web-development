Mario

Mario is a fictional character in the Mario video game franchise, owned by Nintendo and created by Japanese video game designer Shigeru Miyamoto. Serving as the company's mascot and the eponymous protagonist of the series, Mario has appeared in over 200 video games since his creation. Depicted as a short, pudgy, Italian plumber who resides in the Mushroom Kingdom, his adventures generally center upon rescuing Princess Peach from the Koopa villain Bowser. His younger brother and sidekick is Luigi. Since his creation, Mario has appeared in over 200 video games, in addition to starring in numerous spin-off comics, television shows, and films.

Luigi

Luigi is a fictional character featured in video games and related media released by Nintendo. Created by game designer Shigeru Miyamoto, Luigi is portrayed as the slightly younger but taller fraternal twin brother of Nintendo's mascot Mario and appears in many games throughout the Mario franchise, often as a sidekick to his brother. Luigi has appeared in over 200 video games since his creation, including many Mario spin-offs and sports games. He is also featured in Mario Kart, Mario Party, and Super Smash Bros. as a playable character. Luigi is portrayed as the more serious and reserved brother of the two, while Mario is more of a joker. Luigi is also more of a coward than his brother, and is often the one to be captured by the game's villain. Luigi has been depicted as a plumber, a doctor, a ghost hunter, a racer, and a businessman.
